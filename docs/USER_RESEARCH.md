## Session 1 — Owner requirements session

**Participant:** RideNShine business owner, represented through the project sponsor  
**Role:** Shop owner/administrator  
**Method:** Requirements discussion  
**Status:** Initial requirements captured; confirm the summary directly with the owner before defence.

### Current process

- Customers drive to the shop and take a servicing serial, call, or send a Facebook message.
- Customer information is mainly kept through normal invoices rather than a searchable database.
- Stock information is maintained in an Excel sheet.
- Customers often call to ask whether a product is available before travelling.
- Parts usually need to be collected and fitted at the shop, so online payment is not the main need.
- Home delivery is arranged manually after a phone conversation and payment, using a local courier.

### Requested capabilities

- A website for the real business.
- Add, remove and update products.
- Mark products as in stock or stock out.
- Maintain workshop service options.
- Post offers.
- Receive service booking requests.
- Publish honest customer feedback and service pictures.

### Product decisions based on the session

- No online payment in the first version.
- Public stock information and direct call-to-order action.
- Booking remains a request until the shop confirms it.
- Protected owner dashboard.
- Moderation before reviews become public.

3. Would checking stock and service information online save you time?
4. Is a booking request with phone confirmation enough, or do you expect immediate online payment?
